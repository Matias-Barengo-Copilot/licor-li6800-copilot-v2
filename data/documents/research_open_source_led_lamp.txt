Open-Source LED Lamp for the LI-6800 Photosynthesis System
Authors: Stinziano J.R. et al. (seven researchers from various Mexican universities spanning agronomy, physics, and plant physiology)
Journal: Applications in Plant Sciences, October 2024
PMC: pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11788902/

ABSTRACT

This research article describes development of a cost-effective, open-source LED lamp compatible with the LI-6800 Portable Photosynthesis System. It addresses the problem that commercial photosynthesis lamps are expensive and use monochromatic LEDs that do not match sunlight's spectral distribution.

HARDWARE DESIGN

LED Technology: Chip-on-board LED (COB-LED) emitting white light at 6500 K color temperature, similar to sunlight spectral distribution.

Control System: Precision LED driver using XTR110 chip, temperature monitoring via NTC thermistor, and active cooling with CPU cooler assembly.

Controller: Arduino-compatible Trinket M0 microcontroller manages fan speed using PID algorithm. Integrates with LI-6800 console through User I/O port for both manual and programmatic control via Python scripts.

Housing: 3D-printable using SLA printing technology.

PERFORMANCE SPECIFICATIONS

- Maximum PPFD output: greater than 4,250 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹ (without diffuser)
- Spectral distribution: 97.6% of emitted light within photosynthetically active range
- Uniformity: Coefficient of variation less than 10% across at least 97% of measurement area
- Calibration coefficient (r²): 0.999 for PPFD prediction

VALIDATION

Authors demonstrated functionality through A-PPFD curves on bean plants, successfully measuring maximum quantum efficiency and respiration rates. Results confirmed the lamp meets quality standards for photosynthesis research.

AVAILABILITY

All designs, software, and calibration procedures are openly released under:
- CERN Open Hardware License v2
- GNU Lesser General Public License v3.0

Available at GitHub repository (see publication for link).

PRACTICAL ADVANTAGES

- Buildable with basic electronics equipment and a 3D printer
- Calibration requires only standard plant science lab equipment
- Provides reliable photosynthesis parameter measurements
- Significantly more cost-effective than commercial alternatives
- Controllable through LI-6800 console or Python scripts
